# Consumer Insights Analytics

# Sprint 11 – Business Recommendations

---

# Executive Summary

This report summarizes the key findings from the Consumer Insights Analytics project. The analysis combined customer demographic information, survey responses, statistical analysis, machine learning models, and interactive Power BI dashboards to understand customer behavior and identify opportunities for improving customer satisfaction and business performance.

The project applied:

- Exploratory Data Analysis
- Descriptive Statistics
- Inferential Statistics
- Regression Analysis
- Customer Segmentation
- Decision Tree Classification
- Bootstrap Analysis
- Interactive Power BI Dashboard

The findings provide actionable recommendations for improving customer experience, increasing brand loyalty, and supporting data-driven decision-making.

---

# Objective

The primary objective of this project was to answer the following business questions:

- What drives customer satisfaction?
- Which customer groups are most valuable?
- Which factors influence purchase intent?
- How can marketing campaigns be improved?
- Which customers require additional attention?
- What actions will improve customer retention?

---

# Key Findings

## Customer Satisfaction

The average customer satisfaction score indicates that customers are generally satisfied with the company's products and services.

Regression analysis showed that customer satisfaction is strongly influenced by:

- Product Quality
- Service Quality
- Value for Money

Among these variables, Product Quality was identified as the strongest predictor of overall satisfaction.

---

## Product Quality

Customers consistently rated Product Quality as one of the most important factors affecting their experience.

Higher product quality scores were associated with:

- Higher customer satisfaction
- Increased purchase intent
- Greater brand recommendation

---

## Service Quality

Service Quality demonstrated a positive relationship with customer satisfaction.

Customers receiving better service were more likely to:

- Recommend the brand
- Continue purchasing products
- Express positive feedback

---

## Value for Money

Customers who perceived products as offering good value for money reported higher satisfaction levels.

Pricing strategy plays a significant role in customer retention.

---

# Customer Segmentation Findings

K-Means clustering identified three major customer groups.

## Cluster 1 – Price Sensitive Customers

Characteristics

- Highly influenced by pricing
- Moderate satisfaction
- Low brand loyalty

Recommendations

- Offer discounts
- Loyalty rewards
- Seasonal promotions

---

## Cluster 2 – Premium Customers

Characteristics

- High income
- High satisfaction
- Strong preference for premium products

Recommendations

- Premium memberships
- Exclusive offers
- Personalized communication

---

## Cluster 3 – Brand Loyal Customers

Characteristics

- Repeat customers
- High recommendation scores
- High purchase intent

Recommendations

- Referral programs
- Brand ambassador campaigns
- VIP customer benefits

# Decision Tree Insights

The Decision Tree model identified the most influential variables for predicting customer satisfaction.

The most important features were:

- Product Quality
- Service Quality
- Value for Money
- Age

Business implication:

Customers with lower ratings in these variables should be prioritized for customer engagement and service improvements.
